# ParaReport Local Memory

Kolkata civic context:
- Para means neighborhood-level civic memory. Reports should help residents, volunteers, and ward-level responders see repeat problems.
- KMC-style department hints should stay broad and practical: Sewerage and Drainage, Solid Waste Management, Roads, Lighting, Health, Water Supply, Fire, Police, Environment, Parks, Ward Office, Pollution Control.
- ParaReport does not file official complaints. It prepares complaint-ready text and public receipts.

Seasonal memory:
- March-May: heat stress, water pressure, tanker queues, broken taps, outdoor worker risk.
- April-June: pre-monsoon pruning, desilting, loose wires, hoardings, branches, storm preparation.
- June-September: waterlogging, blocked drains, dengue, mosquitoes, open manholes, electricity-water combinations.
- September-October: Pujo temporary city: pandals, bamboo, exits, crowd flow, temporary wiring, food stall waste, route obstruction.
- October-November: post-Pujo dismantling, leftover flex, bamboo, thermocol, drain blockage, road and footpath restoration.
- November-February: road dust, open burning, construction debris, fog, poor visibility.
- All year: East Kolkata Wetlands, bheris, khals, canal pollution, dumping, filling, fires, encroachment.

Multilingual memory:
- Common Bengali/Roman clues: jol/jal/water, joma jol, nala/nordoma, mosha, dengue, khola manhole, tar/wire, alo/streetlight, rasta, goli, bheri, khal, aborjona.
- Common Hindi/Roman clues: pani jama, nala band, machhar, bijli ka taar, sadak, gaddha, kachra, dhuaan, paani nahi, bheed.
- Treat code-mixed reports as normal. A report like "school er shamne drain-e jol jome mosquito hocche" means stagnant drain water near a school and mosquito breeding.

Output memory:
- Bengali share text should be short enough for WhatsApp.
- Hindi share text should be short and readable.
- Official English complaint should include location, issue, risk, and requested action.
